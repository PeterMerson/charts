The three charts below are drawn entirely from Riveredge's own audited annual financial statements. They show the building's complete financial history from 1990 through 2025 — 36 years of data that many shareholders and board members may never have seen in one place. Each chart tells a distinct part of the story.

Chart 1 of 3  ·  Cash & Equivalents Year-End
Total Liquidity has grown 580% since 2005 — and sits 2.5× above the accountant's recommended ceiling.
The building's year-end cash position stands at $2,135,505 in 2025, up from $313,914 at year-end 2005. The two dashed lines show the reserve range the cooperative's own accountant recommended at the annual meeting: 4–6 months of operating expenses, recalculated each year from audited operating expenses. For 2025 that range is $564,391 – $846,586. Current cash is 2.5× the top of that range. The dip in 2022–2024 was driven by a one-time $228,074 fire/water casualty event — and fully recovered in 2025.
Chart 2 of 3  ·  Annual Net Income (Income minus All Expenses incl. Mortgage Principal)
The building has run a surplus in 27 of 36 years. 2024 was the only deficit since 2005 — and 2025 fully reversed it.
Green bars are surplus years; red bars are deficit years. 18 of the last 19 years have been in the black. The 2024 deficit (the single red bar on the right) was caused entirely by a one-time $228,074 fire and water damage event — not by structural financial weakness. 2025 returned to surplus immediately, without any assessment, following that year's record-high maintenance increase of 17%. The orange dashed line marks the landmark 2006 surplus of $257,969 — the year of the 17% maintenance increase. Click any bar to re-read the shareholder memo and view the vote count for that year.
Chart 3 of 3  ·  Capital Expenditures — Major Building Investments by Year
$2.73 million invested in the building since 2006 — funded entirely from operations, without a single assessment.
The building has consistently invested in itself: roofing, boilers, pool, elevators, landscaping, and more. This ongoing reinvestment has not depleted reserves — liquidity has continued to grow. The two cumulative lines compare two distinct periods: blue = 1990–2005 ($1,920,214) and gold = 2006–2025 ($2,730,738), showing how capital investment has grown in the modern era.

✓ Summary — What These Three Charts Show Together

The building's liquidity has grown 457% over 36 years and currently sits 2.5 times above the ceiling recommended by the cooperative's own accountant. The building has generated a surplus in 27 of 36 years, including 18 of the last 19, and returned to surplus in 2025 without any assessment. Over the same period, $4.65 million was invested in capital improvements. Taken together, these charts demonstrate a building in sound financial health that is fully capable of funding its ongoing needs through normal operations. A special assessment of $1,020,324 is not supported by the financial record.
