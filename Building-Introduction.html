What it contains

Five sections. One complete picture.

Each section is fully interactive — select any year range from 1990 to 2024, toggle % and $ changes visible, switch between line and bar charts, and click any row to open a detailed trend chart in the side panel.

→
Income
Where the money comes from — maintenance charges, parking, laundry, storage, interest, transfer fees, and more. Special assessments shown separately and excluded from totals.
→
Expenses
Where it goes — real estate taxes, utilities, payroll, mortgage interest, repairs & maintenance, insurance, security, management fees, water & sewer, legal & audit, and more.
→
Net Income
The surplus or deficit each year — total income minus total expenses. Includes a side-by-side Total Income vs Total Expenses comparison chart. Per-share values shown on each bar.
→
Capital Expenditures
What was invested in the physical building year by year — from major systems replacements to infrastructure improvements. Two periods tracked: 1990–2005 and 2006–2024.
→
Total Liquidity
Cash & equivalents plus reserve fund — the cooperative's financial cushion. Benchmarked against the Westchester "solid adequate" standard of $3,442–$4,917 per unit.
The most personal feature

Your apartment. Your numbers.

🏢 My Unit Calculator
Select your apartment — everything changes.
Every building in a co-op carries expenses collectively, but each shareholder's share is determined by the number of shares attached to their apartment — set in the original 1981 Offering Plan and never changed. Riveredge has 85,027 total shares across 157 apartments ranging from 337 to 706 shares each.

Select your apartment from the dropdown and turn on My Unit View. Every single figure in every matrix instantly recalculates to show your proportional share — Income, Expenses, Net Income, Capital Expenditures, and Total Liquidity. Change the year range and it updates live.

The Westchester standard for a "solid adequate" cooperative reserve is $3,442–$4,917 per unit (2024 dollars, CPI-adjusted). Tools & controls

Everything you can switch on.

Every control is designed to answer a specific question. Nothing is decorative.

Year Range
Year Range Selector
Focus on any period from 1990 to 2024. Want to see just the last 10 years? The period before and after 2006 maintenance increases? The pandemic years? Set From and To and every table and chart updates instantly.
My Unit View
My Unit View
Scales every figure in every matrix to your apartment's proportional share based on shares owned ÷ 85,027 total shares. Turns building-level totals into personally meaningful numbers. Works across all four sections simultaneously.
Compare Apts
Compare Apartments
Opens a scrollable grid showing all 157 apartments sorted by share count. Each card shows 2024 expense allocation, annual and monthly maintenance, liquidity share, percentage of building, and Westchester benchmark status. Your selected unit is highlighted with a star.
% Change
% Change Badges
Shows year-over-year percentage change as a small badge below each cell value. Green for increases, red for decreases. Instantly reveals which expense categories are accelerating or moderating over your selected range.
$ Change
Dollar Change Badges
Shows the raw dollar difference year-over-year. In My Unit View, these differences are also scaled to your unit's share — so you see exactly how many more or fewer dollars your apartment contributed compared to the prior year.
Mortgage Principal
Mortgage Principal Toggle
The cooperative carries a mortgage. Principal payments are a real cash obligation but are excluded from operating expense totals by accounting convention. Toggle this on to see the full cash requirement — the true cost of running the building each year.
CPI-U Overlay
CPI-U Inflation Overlay
Adds a dashed gold inflation line to any trend chart, indexed to the start year of your selected range. See whether an expense category has grown faster or slower than general inflation over any period. Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics All Urban Consumers index.
Chart Type
Line / Bar Chart Toggle
Switch any trend chart between a smooth line (best for showing trajectory and momentum) and a bar chart (best for comparing individual year values). The Net Income fullscreen bar chart labels each bar with both the year and the per-share dollar amount.
Print / Export PDF
Print & Export to PDF
Opens a clean dedicated print window containing all four sections as formatted tables — respecting your current year range, My Unit View setting, and Mortgage Principal toggle. Wide year ranges are automatically split into readable page-width chunks. Use Chrome or Edge's "Save as PDF" to create a shareable document.
Expand ⤢
Fullscreen Chart Expand
Every trend chart in the side panel has a small expand button. Click it to open the chart fullscreen with boosted line weights, larger point sizes, and — in bar mode — year labels and per-share dollar amounts printed directly on each bar. Press Escape or ✕ to close.
