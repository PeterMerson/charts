📄 Shareholder Memorandum — The 2006 Maintenance Increase

TO: Fellow Shareholders, Riveredge Cooperative
FROM: Peter Merson, Shareholder, Apt. 8T
DATE: March 11, 2026
RE: Opposition to Proposed $1,000,000 Special Assessment & The 2006 Maintenance Increase — Setting the Record Straight

PART I: Opposition to the Proposed $1,000,000 Special Assessment (2026)

The Board of Directors intends to levy a special assessment of $1,000,000 upon shareholders beginning July 2026, purportedly to fund a new roof and boiler replacement. I formally oppose this assessment on the grounds that it is financially unnecessary, unsupported by the building's actual fiscal position, and contrary to the best interests of shareholders.

Current Financial Position

  • Cash & Equivalents: $1,976,793
  • Reserve Fund: $0 (fully consolidated into cash position)
  • Total Liquidity: approximately $2,000,000

Reserve Fund vs. Industry Standards

The industry standard for Westchester County co-operatives is 15%–25% of annual operating expenses. Our 2024 adjusted operating expenses total approximately $1,752,000.

  • Recommended reserve (25%): $438,000
  • Our actual liquidity: $2,000,000
  • Our reserve at 114% of operating expenses — more than four times the recommended level

A $1,000,000 assessment across 158 units = $6,329 per shareholder — unnecessary when reserves can fully fund this work. Even after the full project cost, $1,000,000 remains in reserves — still 57% of annual operating expenses.

PART II: The 2006 Maintenance Increase — Setting the Record Straight

I have compiled and analyzed 35 years of audited annual financial statements for Riveredge Cooperative (1990–2024).

The $2.25 Per Share Assessment (2002–2006)

From 2002 through 2006, shareholders paid a $2.25 per share assessment in addition to regular maintenance. When the Board imposed the 17% maintenance increase in 2006, shareholders were told: (1) it was needed for "spiraling operating expenses," and (2) the $2.25 per share assessment would be eliminated. Shareholders believed they were receiving relief. They were not.

What the Audited Records Actually Show

  • Total operating expenses 2005: $1,360,057
  • Total operating expenses 2006: $1,326,987
  • Operating expenses DECREASED by $33,070 from 2005 to 2006
  • Maintenance income jumped from $1,184,580 to $1,436,277 — an increase of $251,697 in one year
  • The $2.25 assessment was never eliminated — it was absorbed into the inflated maintenance charge and continued collecting, year after year

The Per Share Surplus — 2006 Through 2024

  • Total surplus above expenses (2006–2024): $4,205,345
  • Total surplus per share over 19 years: $49.46
  • Average annual surplus per share: $2.60 — virtually identical to the $2.25 assessment shareholders were told had been eliminated

The Real Reason: A Treasury Shortfall

  • A $350,000 loan — borrowed from shareholders within the building — was due in 2006
  • Cash & Equivalents at year-end 2005: $313,914 — a shortfall of ~$36,000
  • This shortfall was the direct result of poor financial management by the Co-op Treasurer

Sworn Testimony That Contradicts What Shareholders Were Told

This matter has previously been the subject of legal proceedings. In sworn testimony under oath, the Co-op President provided an account of the 2006 maintenance increase that was entirely inconsistent with what shareholders had been told. 35 years of audited financial records stand in direct contradiction to that sworn testimony. Operating expenses did not spiral. They decreased. The $2.25 assessment was not eliminated. It was repackaged.

Conclusion

  • Oppose the $1,000,000 special assessment — reserves are more than adequate
  • Demand full financial transparency from the Board
  • Acknowledge that the 2006 increase was not driven by spiraling expenses — directly contradicted by audited records
  • Recognize that $49.46 per share has been collected above expenses since 2006, embedded in artificially inflated maintenance charges

We deserve a board that manages our money with honesty, transparency, and accountability. That is what I have always stood for, and that is why I am putting this on the permanent record.

Peter Merson
Apartment 8T · Riveredge Cooperative · 1-3 David Lane, Yonkers, NY 10710
