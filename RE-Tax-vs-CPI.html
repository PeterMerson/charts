Estimated cumulative RE tax savings vs. CPI-adjusted trajectory: ~$3.6 million (1990–2024)

Had Riveredge's real estate taxes simply grown at the rate of general inflation (CPI), the building would have paid an estimated $14.75 million in taxes over 35 years. Actual taxes paid: $11.12 million. The difference — approximately $3.6 million — was saved through successful tax appeals and abatement challenges, returning roughly $42.67 per share to shareholders over 35 years ($1.22/share/year on average).

1997 appeal saved ~$191K that year

⚠   Notable dips in 1997 (index ~51), 2009 (index ~113) and 2021 (index ~90) reflect successful tax appeals/abatements — confirmed by elevated Legal & Audit fees in those same years. These three campaigns alone saved approximately $637,000 in a single year each. The ongoing discipline of keeping taxes below the CPI trajectory accounts for the remaining ~$3 million in cumulative savings.

CPI data: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics via Federal Reserve Bank of Minneapolis (CPI-U, 1982–84 = 100). RE Tax data: Riveredge audited annual financial statements 1990–2024.
