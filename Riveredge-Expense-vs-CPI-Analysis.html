From 1990 to 2024, the US Consumer Price Index rose +140.4% — meaning a basket of goods costing $1,000 in 1990 cost $2,404 in 2024. Riveredge total operating expenses grew only +72.8% over the same period, saving shareholders $975,686 versus a CPI-adjusted baseline. The dominant driver was a massive decline in Mortgage Interest as the building's debt was paid down. However, Insurance, Water & Sewer, Utilities and Repairs & Maintenance all significantly outpaced inflation and represent the primary cost pressures facing the cooperative.
